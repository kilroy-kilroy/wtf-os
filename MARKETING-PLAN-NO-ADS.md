# WTF Growth OS: No-Ads Marketing Plan

## Executive Summary

This plan leverages Tim Kilroy's subject-matter authority, the product's built-in freemium lead magnets, and community-driven distribution to grow WTF Growth OS without spending a dollar on paid advertising. The core thesis: **the product IS the marketing** -- every free tool analysis is a conversion event, every insight shared is a trust deposit, and every customer result is proof that compounds.

---

## 1. Product-Led Growth (PLG) -- The Engine

### 1A. The 5-Door Funnel is Already Marketing

Your existing architecture is a marketing machine. Each free tool (Call Lab Lite, Discovery Lab Lite, WTF Assessment) generates:

- **A moment of value** the user didn't have before
- **A data-driven reason** to upgrade (personalized upgrade page using Lite results)
- **A shareable artifact** (PDF reports, scores, insights)

**Action items:**

- Add a "Share My Score" CTA to every Lite report output (Twitter/LinkedIn card with score + anonymized insight)
- Build a "Team Challenge" mechanic: "Get your whole sales team scored -- see who's really closing"
- Add a benchmark comparison: "You scored X. Top 10% of agencies score Y. Here's the gap."
- Every PDF report footer: "Analyzed by WTF Growth OS -- get your free analysis at [URL]"

### 1B. Viral Loops Built Into the Product

- **Call Lab Lite**: After analysis, prompt users to "Analyze your competitor's public sales calls" -- drives new signups
- **Discovery Lab Lite**: Offer a "Send this brief to your team" share that requires recipient signup
- **WTF Assessment**: Create a "Compare your agency to 500+ others" benchmark that requires participation (signup) to see results

## 2. Content as Distribution -- Tim Kilroy's Authority Platform

### 2A. The "WTF Sales Breakdown" Series (LinkedIn + YouTube)

Tim picks apart real sales moments (anonymized or with permission) using the Human-First Sales framework. This is NOT generic sales advice -- it's the methodology in action.

**Format options:**

- **LinkedIn text posts** (2-3x/week): Short, punchy breakdowns of a single sales moment. "The rep asked 'What's your budget?' at minute 3. Here's why the deal died at minute 47."
- **YouTube/Loom videos** (1x/week): 5-8 min teardowns of call patterns, using Call Lab Pro's actual scoring dimensions (Emotional Safety, Insight & Reframing, Trust Acceleration)
- **LinkedIn carousel** (1x/week): "5 things I learned analyzing 200 sales calls this month" -- data-driven, from actual platform usage

**Why this works without ads:** The content demonstrates the product's value by showing the methodology. Viewers think "I want my calls analyzed like that" -- the CTA is always "Get your free Call Lab Lite analysis."

### 2B. The "WTF Weekly" Newsletter

A weekly email with:

1. **One anonymized call breakdown** (showcasing Call Lab Pro's analysis depth)
2. **One contrarian sales insight** backed by aggregate platform data
3. **One tool tip** showing a non-obvious way to use the free tools
4. **Community spotlight** -- a user win or transformation story

**Growth mechanics:**
- Gated behind email signup (obviously)
- "Forward to a sales leader who needs this" CTA in every issue
- Referral program: refer 3 subscribers, get one free Call Lab Pro analysis

### 2C. "The WTF Sales Method" Mini-Course (Lead Magnet)

A 5-email drip sequence teaching the Human-First Sales methodology:

1. Why customers pick the person first, deal second
2. Trust architecture vs. tactical manipulation
3. The vulnerability framework -- creating permission for truth
4. Insight over interrogation -- reframing as a superpower
5. Putting it together -- with a Call Lab Lite analysis of YOUR call

Each email ends with a specific free tool CTA. Email 5 is the conversion moment.

## 3. Community & Partnerships -- Borrowed Audiences

### 3A. The "WTF Sales Lab" Community

A free Slack/Discord community for sales leaders and agency owners.

**Content pillars inside the community:**
- Weekly "Hot Seat" where a member submits a call for live breakdown (Tim or AI-assisted)
- Monthly "Benchmark Report" -- aggregate data from Call Lab analyses shared with the community
- Peer accountability pods (3-5 people sharing calls and coaching each other using the platform)

**Why this works:** Community members become power users. Power users become evangelists. Evangelists bring their networks.

### 3B. Podcast Guesting Circuit

Tim appears on podcasts targeting:

- Agency owners (Agency Leadership podcasts)
- B2B sales leaders (Sales Hacker, Predictable Revenue, etc.)
- SaaS founders (for the PLG story)

**The pitch to podcast hosts:** "I've analyzed X thousand sales calls with AI. Here are the patterns that separate closers from pretenders." -- This is inherently interesting content that hosts want.

**Always bring a gift:** Offer every podcast's audience a custom landing page with extended free access or a bonus analysis.

### 3C. Strategic Integration Partnerships

- **Call recording platforms** (Fireflies, Gong, Chorus): "Already recording calls? Analyze them with WTF." Integration partnerships where they recommend you in their ecosystem.
- **CRM platforms**: Build lightweight integrations and get listed in partner directories/marketplaces.
- **Sales training companies**: White-label or co-brand the assessment tools. They get a lead magnet; you get distribution.

## 5. Social Proof & Case Studies -- Let Users Sell for You

### 5A. The "Before/After" Machine

Track users from first Lite analysis through Pro usage. Document:

- Starting Call Lab score vs. score after 90 days of coaching
- Revenue impact: deals closed before vs. after
- Time saved: hours spent on manual call review vs. automated

**Format:** Short case study cards (LinkedIn-friendly), detailed case studies on the site, video testimonials.

### 5B. User-Generated Content Triggers

- After every Pro analysis, prompt: "Did this insight help you close? Tell us in 30 seconds" (video or text)
- Create a "Wall of Wins" on the site -- real quotes from real users
- Annual "WTF Sales Awards" -- gamified recognition for top-improving users

### 5C. Data-Driven PR

Publish quarterly reports using aggregate platform data:

- "The State of Agency Sales Calls: Q1 2026"
- "The #1 Mistake Sales Teams Make in Discovery Calls (Based on 5,000 Analyses)"

Pitch these to trade publications (HubSpot blog, Sales Hacker, Agency-focused media). The data is unique to your platform -- no one else has it.

## Phased Rollout

### Phase 1: Foundation (Now - Month 2)
- Ship share/viral mechanics in Lite tools
- Launch LinkedIn content cadence (3x/week)
- Start newsletter with existing email list
- Set up community (Slack or Discord)
- Build 3 SEO landing pages around free tools

### Phase 2: Amplify (Months 3-5)
- Begin podcast guesting (2-3/month)
- Launch referral program
- Publish first data report
- First virtual workshop/event
- Start ambassador outreach

### Phase 3: Compound (Months 6-12)
- Integration partnerships go live
- Community hits critical mass and becomes self-sustaining
- SEO content starts ranking and driving organic signups
- Case studies and social proof library is robust
- Speaking circuit is generating consistent inbound

## The Bottom Line

You don't need ads because:

1. **Your free tools are better marketing than any ad.** A Call Lab Lite analysis that reveals a real insight is worth more than 10,000 impressions.
2. **Tim's methodology is inherently shareable.** Contrarian, data-backed sales insights spread on their own.
3. **Every user is a potential distribution channel.** Shareable reports, team challenges, and referral mechanics turn users into marketers.
4. **The data moat grows with usage.** The more calls analyzed, the better the benchmarks, the more valuable the content, the more users you attract. Flywheel.

The goal is not to "get attention." The goal is to **be so useful that attention finds you**.
